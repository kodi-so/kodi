# Kodi Design Context

## Product

Kodi is a managed AI teammate platform for modern teams. Every team gets their own dedicated AI instance that joins calls, listens to plans and decisions, answers questions with business context, tracks action items, and carries follow-through work across connected tools.

## Target Audience

Employees and teams who coordinate work across meetings, chat, ticketing systems, and internal tools. Not necessarily technical. Time-poor. Frustrated by lost context, scattered action items, and manual follow-up work. Want help moving from conversation to execution without adding more process.

## Brand Personality

- **Confident and direct** — no fluff, no corpo-speak
- **Capable without being intimidating** — feels powerful but approachable
- **Modern but not trendy** — should feel current in 3 years, not just today
- **Trustworthy** — handles sensitive business data, so reliability matters

## Design Tone

Premium SaaS with editorial sensibility. Think Linear meets Notion meets a well-designed finance tool. Clean, purposeful, every element earns its place. NOT: generic AI startup with purple gradients and glowing orbs.

## Key Pages

- **Landing page** (`apps/web`) — marketing/conversion, single page
- **App** (`apps/app`) — authenticated product UI with login, dashboard, feed

## Technical Stack

- Next.js (App Router), Tailwind CSS, TypeScript
- No component library (custom UI in `@kodi/ui`)
- Standalone Docker builds deployed on Railway

## What to Avoid

- Purple-to-indigo gradients (current design leans too hard on this)
- Gradient text on headings
- Inter/system fonts — needs a more distinctive type choice
- Cards nested in cards
- Generic "AI startup" dark mode aesthetic
- Identical card grids with icon + heading + text repeated

## What Would Make This Memorable

A landing page that feels like it was made by people who actually understand how work happens across meetings, chat, and tickets — not a Silicon Valley AI demo. Confident copy, clean hierarchy, and a design that doesn't look like every other "AI for [category]" landing page.
