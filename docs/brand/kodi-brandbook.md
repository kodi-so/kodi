# Kodi Brandbook

Status: Approved
Prepared: April 3, 2026

## 1. Brand Objective

Kodi should feel like the AI teammate lean teams actually trust with real work and gradually delegate more of that work to over time:

- clear, not abstract
- capable, not flashy
- warm, not soft
- operational, not enterprise-heavy

For startups and SMBs, the brand has to answer five questions quickly:

1. What is this?
2. Can it actually do the work, not just summarize it?
3. Will it fit my stack and budget?
4. Can I control how autonomous it is?
5. Can I trust it with follow-through?

## 2. Brand Core

### Mission

Help lean teams leave every important conversation with clarity, ownership, and less manual work on their plate.

### Vision

Work should move at the speed of conversation. People should spend more time on bigger-picture, system-level thinking while Kodi handles more of the nitty-gritty execution inside the guardrails they choose.

### Brand Promise

Kodi turns conversations into completed work, at the autonomy level you choose.

### Category

AI teammate for meetings, execution, and controlled autonomy.

Preferred sharper category language for headlines and sales:

- meeting execution agent
- shared AI teammate for team operations
- controlled-autonomy execution agent

Avoid leading with:

- note taker
- meeting bot
- copilot
- productivity platform

### Positioning Statement

Kodi is the AI teammate for startups and SMBs that need meetings to end with work already moving or already done. It joins the conversation, answers with live business context, and can organize, draft, route, or execute follow-through across the tools and accounts users connect, all within user-defined autonomy settings.

## 3. Who Kodi Is For

### Primary customer profile

Lean teams from roughly 5 to 250 people that operate through frequent decision-heavy conversations and cannot afford coordination drag.

### Best-fit buyers

- founders who are still in too many critical meetings
- operations leaders who own process quality and handoffs
- team leads who need decisions to become visible work fast

### Core customer pains

- important decisions disappear into memory or messy notes
- nobody owns the recap, so no one trusts the recap
- answers to live questions are scattered across Slack, docs, CRM, and ticketing
- leaders become the default router for every small follow-up task
- lean teams do not want another system to feed
- too much nitty-gritty execution still stays with humans after the plan is already clear
- seat-based pricing punishes adoption

### What startups and SMBs care about most

- fast setup
- obvious ROI within one or two meetings
- compatibility with their current stack
- shared team value, not individual novelty
- confidence that AI can take work off the team's plate without going off the rails
- control over writes and approvals
- pricing that does not scale painfully with headcount

## 4. Strategic Brand Angle

Kodi should not compete on "smartest AI."

Kodi should compete on "most trusted delegated operator in the room."

That means the brand emphasis is:

- presence: Kodi is there when decisions happen
- clarity: Kodi makes the room sharper, not noisier
- continuity: work keeps moving after the meeting ends
- execution: Kodi can take work off the team's plate, not just point at the work
- control: writes are governed and reviewable
- progression: teams can expand Kodi's autonomy over time as trust grows
- practicality: it works with the tools teams already use

## 5. Differentiation

### Controlled autonomy model

Kodi should be framed as a progressive delegation system, not a binary assistant.

The autonomy ladder:

- organize: capture decisions, summarize, map owners, and clarify next steps
- prepare: draft tasks, messages, docs, recaps, and updates before they are sent
- act with approval: execute sensitive work after a user reviews the action
- act within guardrails: complete approved classes of work autonomously through connected tools and accounts

Brand implication:

Kodi grows from organizer to operator as trust grows. The user always decides the boundaries.

### What Kodi is not

- not just a transcript or summary tool
- not another dashboard to maintain
- not a one-size-fits-all autopilot
- not enterprise software built for giant procurement cycles

### What Kodi is

- a shared agent for the team, not a toy for one person
- an execution layer that starts inside the meeting
- a live context layer across business tools
- a follow-through system with clear ownership, execution, and approvals
- an agent that can complete work itself through connected tools and accounts
- a user-controlled autonomy system that expands as trust grows

### Competitive wedge

Most meeting AI products stop at notes.

Most workflow automation products start after the meeting.

Most autonomy tools lack the live context of the room and the trust layer teams need.

Kodi owns the chain from conversation to controlled execution: when a team is deciding what to do, who owns it, what should happen next, and which parts Kodi should carry itself.

Kodi joins meetings, answers with live business context, and can organize, draft, approve-route, or execute follow-through across your stack at the autonomy level your team chooses.

## 10. Brand Narrative

### The before state

Lean teams spend real time in high-value conversations, but the output is fragile:

- notes are incomplete
- decisions are not durable
- owners are fuzzy
- follow-up happens late or not at all
- leaders still end up doing the nitty-gritty execution themselves

### The after state

With Kodi, the meeting becomes the start of execution:

- decisions are captured while they happen
- questions get answered with business context
- next steps are prepared or executed immediately
- the team leaves the room with momentum

### Emotional payoff

Kodi should make teams feel:

- more in control
- less dependent on memory
- faster without becoming chaotic
- supported, not replaced
- freer to focus on system-level thinking
- more confident delegating operational detail

## 11. Visual Identity

### Brand idea

The attached Kodi mark should represent presence, listening, and focus.

Its visual meaning:

- centered form = calm presence
- side dots = listening points / signal / context around the core
- soft geometry = approachable intelligence

### Logo system

Primary recommendation:

- use the attached logo mark as the primary symbol across web, app, favicon, and social avatars
- pair it with a custom wordmark set in ABeeZee for the full brand lockup
- retire the placeholder gradient square with the letter `K`

Logo usage rules:

- default mark color: Kodi Ink
- use the mark on clean, quiet surfaces
- prefer one-color applications for everyday product use
- use reversed white mark on dark product surfaces
- do not distort, rotate, or over-decorate the mark
- do not swap the mark for a letter badge except in true fallback cases

Clear space:

- minimum clear space around the mark should equal the diameter of one side dot

## 12. Typography

### Primary typeface

ABeeZee is the main Kodi brand font.

Use ABeeZee for:

- wordmark
- hero headlines
- section headlines
- major marketing statements
- key UI headings and empty states

### Supporting typeface

Use IBM Plex Sans or a similar neutral sans for dense product text where readability matters more than personality:

- tables
- settings forms
- secondary labels
- long body copy in the app

### Type rules

- let size, spacing, and layout create hierarchy instead of relying on many font weights
- keep headlines short and direct
- avoid overly condensed all-caps treatments
- keep UI labels crisp and sentence case

## 13. Color System

The current best direction is a two-world system:

- warm light brand surfaces for marketing
- deep slate product surfaces for the app

This keeps Kodi approachable in acquisition while preserving focus and control in-product.

### Core colors

| Token | Hex | Role |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Kodi Ink | `#3E5056` | Primary brand color, logo, primary text on light surfaces |
| Room Dark | `#223239` | Deep background for app and dark sections |
| Canvas | `#F6F4EE` | Main light background for marketing |
| Chalk | `#FFFFFF` | Elevated cards and reverse surfaces |
| Warm Sand | `#E8E0D1` | Secondary light surface / borders / soft fills |
| Signal Gold | `#DFAE56` | Primary accent, CTA emphasis, highlights |
| Moss | `#6FA88C` | Success / active / live status |
| Coral | `#D97A63` | Risk / warning / action-needed state |
| Mist Line | `#C9D2D4` | Dividers, quiet borders, interface scaffolding |

### Palette strategy

Kodi Ink is the anchor.

Signal Gold provides motion and commercial energy.

Canvas and Warm Sand keep the marketing site human and approachable.

Room Dark gives the app a focused operating-room feel without falling into generic black SaaS styling.

### Color usage rules

- use Kodi Ink as the brand constant across both light and dark contexts
- reserve Signal Gold for emphasis, CTA, and important highlights
- use Moss for success and "live" states
- use Coral sparingly for warnings and risk
- avoid neon gradients and purple-heavy AI styling
- avoid pure black and pure blue as dominant brand anchors

## 14. UI and Product Design Direction

### Overall feel

Kodi should feel like a calm control room for team execution.

### Design principles

- quiet confidence
- high legibility
- soft geometry
- clear statefulness
- visible momentum

### UI traits to standardize

- rounded shapes that echo the logo geometry
- warm-light marketing and slate-dark product environments
- focused accent usage
- card systems with clear hierarchy
- statuses that explain what Kodi is doing now and what happens next
- visible autonomy states so teams understand what Kodi will draft, request approval for, or execute directly

### What to remove over time

- generic indigo-to-purple gradients
- the square `K` placeholder brand mark
- copy that sounds like a generic AI SaaS template

## 15. Imagery and Illustration Direction

Use imagery that shows:

- real teams in working sessions
- close crops of collaborative moments
- product screens with visible decisions, owners, and next steps
- diagrams that show flow from conversation to action

Avoid:

- humanoid robots
- glowing brain art
- abstract mesh gradients with no meaning
- stock imagery that looks enterprise-polished and emotionally empty

Illustration style:

- minimal
- rounded
- signal-based
- operational, not whimsical

## 16. Motion Direction

Motion should communicate continuity and follow-through.

Good motion cues:

- conversation becomes action
- item moves from "captured" to "approved" to "executed"
- soft pulses for live state
- staggered reveals for system confidence

Avoid:

- decorative floatiness
- sci-fi motion language
- motion that competes with legibility

## 20. Non-Negotiable Brand Rules

1. Kodi is never framed as just a note taker.
2. Kodi is always described as an agent that can organize, draft, and execute work, not a novelty layer.
3. Users control what Kodi can and cannot do, and how autonomous it is.
4. Messaging should connect conversation, context, execution, and follow-through.
5. The attached logo mark replaces the generic `K` as the primary symbol.
6. ABeeZee is the brand-defining typeface.
7. Purple-led AI aesthetics should be phased out.
8. Every important marketing surface should make the startup/SMB value obvious:
   fast setup, shared value, controlled autonomy, and team pricing.
